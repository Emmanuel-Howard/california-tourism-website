The City of Angels


Los Angeles, the entertainment capital of the world, is a dynamic metropolis known for its cultural diversity, stunning beaches, and thriving arts scene. Home to Hollywood, LA has long been the epicenter of the film and television industry, attracting dreamers and creators from around the globe. With its iconic skyline, palm-lined streets, and year-round sunshine, the city offers an unmatched blend of urban energy and laid-back California vibes.

From the luxury of Beverly Hills to the bohemian spirit of Venice Beach, every neighborhood tells a unique story, making Los Angeles one of the most exciting places to visit and live.

Los Angeles at a Glance


As the largest city in California, Los Angeles boasts a population of nearly 4 million residents and was officially incorporated in 1850. It is located in Southern California and is classified as a highly urban city with sprawling suburban regions extending into the Greater LA area. The city serves as an economic powerhouse, hosting major industries in entertainment, technology, and international trade.

While LA is home to some of the most affluent communities in the nation, the city's average income level remains slightly below the state average due to its vast population and income disparities. Despite this, Los Angeles continues to be a land of opportunity, innovation, and cultural influence.
